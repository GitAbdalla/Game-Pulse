Counter-Strike, often abbreviated as CS,
is a highly popular and influential first-person shooter (FPS) video game
that has left an indelible mark on the gaming industry since its inception.
Developed by Valve Corporation and Hidden Path Entertainment,
the game first emerged as a mod for Half-Life in 1999 before evolving into a standalone title
with Counter-Strike 1.6
and subsequently Counter-Strike: Source. However,
it was Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO), released in 2012,
that truly solidified the franchise's status as a competitive gaming juggernaut.

Minecraft, created by Markus Persson and initially released in 2011, is a sandbox game that has become a cultural phenomenon, captivating players of all ages around the world. This open-world game allows players to explore and interact with a blocky, procedurally-generated 3D world, where they can build and craft using various materials and resources. One of Minecraft's defining features is its limitless creativity. Players can construct elaborate structures, intricate landscapes, and even functioning mechanisms using the game's simple yet versatile building blocks. The game offers different modes, including Survival mode, where players must gather resources and fend off hostile creatures, and Creative mode, which provides unlimited resources for unrestricted building.

League of Legends (LoL), developed by Riot Games and first released in 2009, is a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game that has become a cornerstone of the esports industry and one of the most played games in the world. LoL has played a significant role in shaping the competitive gaming landscape and has amassed a massive and dedicated player base. In League of Legends, players assume the role of "champions," each with unique abilities, strengths, and weaknesses. The game is played in teams of five, and the objective is to destroy the opposing team's Nexus, a core building located within their base. The strategic depth of the game lies in champion selection, teamwork, map awareness, and skillful execution of abilities during matches.

Fortnite, developed by Epic Games and initially released in 2017, is a free-to-play battle royale game that has taken the gaming world by storm. It has become a cultural phenomenon, attracting millions of players worldwide and transcending the boundaries of traditional gaming. The game's most popular mode, Fortnite Battle Royale, follows the battle royale genre's format, where 100 players are dropped onto an island and must fight to be the last person or team standing. The game is known for its unique building mechanics, allowing players to construct structures using materials harvested from the environment. This adds an additional layer of strategy and creativity to the typical battle royale gameplay.
